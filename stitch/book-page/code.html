White Fang

Jack London

White Fang by Jack London is a powerful companion novel to The Call of the Wild, exploring the journey from wildness to civilization. Published in 1906, this gripping tale follows White Fang, a wolf-dog hybrid born in the frozen Yukon wilderness, as he experiences the full spectrum of human cruelty and kindness.

From his early life with a Native American tribe through brutal abuse as a fighting dog to eventual redemption with a loving master, White Fang's transformation illuminates the debate between nature and nurture. London's vivid prose captures both the harsh beauty of the Arctic and the animal's complex inner life. A thrilling adventure with profound themes.
